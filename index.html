About VR

If you have never heard of it, watch this short video:

Whether you're a gamer looking to develop and play games or an educator looking to improve the quality of education your students receive, Virtual Reality can take part in bettering every area of life.

Some hospitals have treated patients using virtual reality headsets; huge advances like this while virtual reality is still a growing industry could mean so much for the future of VR in patient care. Virtual reality could be a huge asset to schools because, for visual learners, seeing their lessons right in front of them could be essential to their growth in school. In the future, we might even be able to have face to face communication with people on opposite sides of the world!

Unreal Engine 4

A First Place Project

Unreal Engine 4 is a game design application that we used to create our experiences and/or games for the first 5 weeks of VR Camp. After watching the VR experience, Henry, we were inspired to create our own short story, which we called Dani and Rudy.

Realizing that we might not have enough time to make a story as graphically detailed as Henry, we decided to approach a different type of format. As you can see, we change the scenes and move the story along by essentially opening up the door to the next scene.

360 Video

360 video is extremely innovative and essential for the further development of virtual reality, and the Ricoh Theta S helped us experiment with 360 video easily. We decided that with the week that we had with the camera, it would be cool to show off parts of San Francisco - one of the most popular tourist hotspots. Check out our 360 video below!

A-Frame

For the last two weeks of camp, we also had the opportunity to learn about a-frame web VR. Web VR exactly what it sounds like, VR that you can share over the web by putting it on your website. Web VR is the most accessible form of virtual reality right now because anyone who has a mobile phone or access to a computer can view it. You can view a very simple example of Web VR below, just to see what it is like.
